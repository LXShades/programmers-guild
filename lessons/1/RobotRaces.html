Fasten your seatbelts for the Robot Races!
In teams of 3-4 you will design and build a super-robot in LEGO MINDSTORMS that will race against the others in a 3-lap loop around the game studio.
During the race, robots must pass several marked checkpoints along the path to be considered eligible for the next lap, with some leniency.
The race commences in Session [x]!

Learning Objectives:
  • Plan a remote-controlled robot, with an aptitude for moving supah-fast and optionally weapon(s) to obstruct other racers
  • Use the agile development philosophy to build and program the robot components within your group
  • Be the very best like no-one ever was
Agenda:
0000 - 0010 Arrival and Registration
0010 - 0140 Robot Construction session
0140 - 0200 Coffee Break
0200 - 0245 General Programming Support and Discussion
0245 - 0250 Debrief
0250 - 0300 Departure

Further Guidance:
  • Plan a remote-controlled robot, with an aptitude for moving supah-fast with optional weapon(s) to obstruct other racers
  • Use Trello or a similar management technique to organise necessary component-building and programming tasks.
  • Using the agile development philosophy, begin iteratively building and programming the robot components within your group
  • Ensure the robot is programmed to be user-controllable from a distance
  • Have a human race or maybe an epic rock-paper scissors battle to decide who drives the robot (or pehaps decide in session [x], such that you can build the robot before potentially being in a passive aggressive war with each other, rather than after)
  • Prepare to race the robots along a pre-set track by session [x].

Further Learning:
  • Read 7 Coolest Robot Wars robots by Joe Roberts for inspiration in gratuitous robotic violence
  • Watch Wacky Races for a long, long list of traps and weapons that probably won't work, courtesy of Dick Dastardly
  • Engage in the passive viewing of cat videos to relax and calm down after a stressful day of robot building
